Evaluation Guide

Abstract Visual Reasoning Evaluation

This document outlines the criteria for evaluating Text-to-Image Model (T2IM) outputs assisted by Large Language Models (LLMs). The goal is to assess the model's ability to translate abstract concepts into coherent visuals, moving beyond literal interpretations to capture the semantic "essence" of the prompt.

Evaluators must grade images on a scale of 1 to 5, assessing both semantic alignment (meaning) and stylistic adherence.

1. Literary Works

Part 1: General Evaluation Rubric

ScoreLevelDefinition
1Literal AssemblageA "what's in the book" collage. No thematic coherence.
2Scene RecreationDepicts a famous scene but misses emotional weight or symbolism.
3Surface ThemeRecognizes a theme (mood) but states it rather than evoking it deeply.
4Layered NarrativeGrasps deeper purpose; expresses multiple themes or climactic meaning.
5Artistic MasteryCaptures what the book feels like to have read. Professional quality.

4. Idioms

Part 1: General Evaluation Rubric

ScoreLevelDefinition
1Literal InterpretationTranslates words directly (e.g., anatomical stomach).
2Vague SubjectivityRecognizes a feeling is involved, but it is generic.
3Emotion / No ContextCorrect specific emotion, but missing environmental trigger.
4Contextual NarrativeVisualizes a scenario that causes the meaning.
5Artistic MasterySeamless integration of context, emotion, and style.
